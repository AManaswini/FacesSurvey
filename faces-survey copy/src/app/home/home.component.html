    A person arrested in the United States faces a judge within 24 hours of arrest. That judge makes an important decision about where this person will wait for trial. Must they sit in jail? Or can they go home? Whether a person is jailed by law is supposed to depend on the risk that person poses: Would they commit a crime?
    Your goal for this exercise is to decide who should be released or detained. To do that, you will be shown a pair of arrest records, including some information about the person and an arrest mugshot. You must select the individual you believe should be kept in jail, according to how likely they are to be re-arrested before their trial.
    Your first 15 choices serve as a training period. For these choices, after making your selection, you will be given feedback to tell if you picked the riskier defendant. There is no time limit for the training period.
    You will then enter an evaluation period. For the evaluation period, you can make up to 45 choices, with a time limit of 5 minutes. After the time limit has expired, your survey will end, and you will be given a completion code. If you do not complete all choices, you will still be given a completion code. For these choices, after making your selection, you will not be given any feedback about the selection
